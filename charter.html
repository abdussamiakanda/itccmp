Institutional Charter
ITCPR / About / Institutional Charter
Preamble
This Charter establishes the Institution for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research, henceforth referred to as "The Institution," dedicated to advancing the field of physics through research, collaboration, and education. This document outlines the foundational principles, governance structure, and operational policies of The Institution.



Article I: Name and Purpose

Name

  • Official Name: The official name of this entity shall be "Institution for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research".
  • Abbreviation: The Institution may also be referred to as "ITCPR" in all official documents and communications.

Purpose

  • Primary Objective: The primary objective of ITCPR is to advance the field of theoretical and computational physics through innovative research and educational initiatives.
  • Research and Innovation: To conduct and promote research in theoretical and computational physics that contributes to the global body of scientific knowledge, pushing the boundaries of understanding in the field.
  • Education and Training: To provide quality education and training programs in theoretical and computational physics, especially aimed at students and researchers from regions with limited access to such opportunities.
  • Collaboration and Community Building: To foster a collaborative and supportive community of physicists, encouraging the exchange of ideas and resources among researchers from different geographical and academic backgrounds.
  • Global Participation: To actively engage in the global scientific dialogue, participating in and contributing to international conferences, seminars, and workshops.
  • Accessibility and Opportunity: To democratize access to advanced research in physics, ensuring that talented individuals from underrepresented or underserved regions have the opportunity to contribute to and benefit from the advancements in the field.

Values and Principles

  • Commitment to Excellence: Upholding the highest standards of research and education in all endeavors.
  • Inclusivity and Diversity: Embracing diversity in all forms and striving for inclusivity in the scientific community.
  • Integrity and Ethics: Adhering to the highest ethical standards in all research and academic activities.
  • Innovation and Creativity: Encouraging innovative thinking and creative approaches to problem-solving in physics research.

Long-term Vision

  • Global Impact: Aspiring to make a significant impact on the global scientific community and contribute to the advancement of knowledge and technology in physics.
  • Sustainable Growth: Aiming for sustainable growth and development of the institution, adapting to the evolving needs of the scientific community and the world.


Article II: Legal Structure

Type of Entity

  • Classification: ITCPR shall be established and operated as a non-profit, non-governmental organization, dedicated to the advancement of theoretical and computational physics research and education.
  • Compliance with Laws: The Institution shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations of the jurisdiction in which it is registered. This includes adherence to legal standards pertaining to non-profit organizations, international collaborations, intellectual property, and academic research.

Registration and Legal Status

  • Jurisdiction: ITCPR shall be legally registered in Bangladesh, where it will maintain its primary legal address.
  • Registration Details: The Institution shall be registered with the Registrar of Joint Stock Companies And Firms (RJSC) as required by law, ensuring its recognition as a legitimate non-governmental educational and research entity.

Location and Operation

  • Principal Office: ITCPR operates as a virtual institution without a fixed physical location. The principal administrative activities are conducted online, enabling global participation and collaboration. A physical office may be established in the future as per the needs and growth of the institution.

Governance Compliance

  • Board of Trustees: Governance and oversight of the Institution shall be the responsibility of a Board of Trustees, which shall ensure that ITCPR operates within its legal and ethical boundaries and adheres to its mission and objectives.
  • Legal and Ethical Standards: The Board shall establish and enforce policies and procedures that uphold the highest legal and ethical standards in all activities of the Institution.

Fiscal Responsibility

  • Financial Management: ITCPR shall maintain transparent and responsible management of its finances, adhering to accounting standards and financial reporting requirements as stipulated by law.
  • Non-profit Integrity: As a non-profit organization, any surplus funds shall be reinvested into the Institution for the furtherance of its objectives, rather than being distributed to members.

Amendments to Legal Structure

  • Procedure for Amendments: Any changes to the legal structure or status of ITCPR must be proposed by the Board of Trustees and approved by a majority vote, following the procedures outlined in Article VI of this Charter.
  • Compliance with Changes in Law: The Institution shall adapt its legal structure as necessary to remain compliant with any changes in the laws and regulations governing non-profit organizations and educational institutions.


Article III: Governance

Executive Directors and Leadership

  • Executive Directors: ITCPR is led by one Executive Director and one Co-Executive Director, who are jointly responsible for setting the vision, strategy, and overall direction of the institution.
  • Dual Roles: Given the early stage of the institution, the Executive Directors will also undertake operational roles, managing day-to-day activities and making key decisions.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Executive Director: Responsible for overall strategic leadership and decision-making, including setting institutional goals and guiding the organization's direction.
  • Co-Executive Director: Works in tandem with the Executive Director to co-lead the organization, focusing on strategic initiatives, partnerships, and organizational development.
  • Trustees: Provide governance and oversight, ensuring that the institution adheres to its mission and ethical standards. Involved in major policy decisions and long-term planning.
  • Research Group Lead: Oversees specific research projects, coordinates the research team, and ensures the quality and integrity of research outputs.
  • Lab Manager: Manages laboratory operations, including equipment maintenance, safety protocols, and logistical support for research activities.
  • Members: Engage in research and educational activities, contributing to the institution's goals and participating in collaborative projects.
  • Interns: Assist in research and administrative tasks while gaining practical experience and training in theoretical and computational physics research.

Meetings and Decision-Making

  • Leadership Meetings: Regular meetings involving the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, and Trustees will be held to discuss organizational progress, make strategic decisions, and plan future initiatives.
  • Research Group Meetings: The Research Group Lead will convene meetings with the research team and lab manager to coordinate on projects, discuss research developments, and address operational needs.
  • Consensus-Based Decisions: Major decisions will be reached through consensus among the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, and Trustees. Structured discussions and inclusive consultation processes will be utilized to address different perspectives and resolve any differences.
  • Member and Intern Involvement: Regular forums or meetings will be organized to involve members and interns in broader discussions, ensuring their perspectives and contributions are considered in decision-making processes.

Growth and Expansion of the Team

  • Future Team Expansion: As ITCPR grows, we anticipate expanding our team to include more specialized roles such as additional research group leads, lab managers, and administrative staff. This growth will be aligned with the increasing scope and scale of our research and educational activities.
  • Structural Evolution: With the expansion, our governance structure will evolve to include a more diverse range of expertise and perspectives, potentially leading to the establishment of a formal advisory board and dedicated committees for specific operational areas.

Transparency and Communication

  • Inclusive Communication: We are committed to maintaining open and inclusive communication channels within our team, ensuring that all members, from executive directors to interns, are informed and engaged in the institution's developments and objectives.
  • Comprehensive Record-Keeping: Detailed records of institutional decisions, research progress, and administrative discussions will be meticulously maintained. This practice not only ensures transparency but also provides valuable historical insights for future reference as ITCPR continues to grow and evolve.


Article IV: Membership

Eligibility for Membership

  • General Criteria: Membership in ITCPR is open to professionals and enthusiasts with a keen interest or background in theoretical and computational physics.
  • Special Consideration: Special emphasis is placed on including individuals from regions with limited access to research opportunities, in line with ITCPR's mission of global scientific inclusion.

Categories of Membership

  • Executive and Co-Executive Directors: Primary leaders responsible for strategic oversight and direction of ITCPR.
  • Trustees: Individuals providing governance and advisory support to the institution.
  • Research Group Leads: Responsible for leading specific research projects and teams.
  • Lab Managers: Overseeing the operation and management of research laboratories.
  • Members: Engaged in contributing to research, education, and other institutional activities.
  • Interns: Gaining experience and training through active participation in various projects and institutional operations.

Admission Process

  • Application and Review: Candidates must apply formally, with applications reviewed by a designated committee.
  • Acceptance: Admission decisions are based on alignment with ITCPR's goals, potential contributions, and institutional capacity.

Rights and Responsibilities of Members

  • Participation: All members are encouraged to actively participate in ITCPR's projects, activities, and decision-making processes.
  • Contribution: Members are expected to contribute positively and ethically to the institution's research endeavors and learning environment.
  • Code of Conduct: Adherence to a high standard of professionalism, ethical conduct, and respect for collaborative work is required from all members.

Termination of Membership

  • Voluntary Resignation: Members can resign from their positions by providing written notice to the Co-Directors.
  • Involuntary Termination: Membership may be revoked for reasons such as misconduct, failure to meet responsibilities, or breach of ITCPR's policies, as determined by the Executive Directors or a designated committee.

Updating Membership Policies

  • Policy Revisions: The membership policies are subject to review and amendment to accommodate the evolving needs of ITCPR and its community.
  • Communication of Changes: Any changes to membership policies will be communicated promptly to all members.


Article V: Administration

Administrative Structure

  • Executive Management: The Executive Director, assisted by the Co-Executive Director, oversees the overall administration and operational management of ITCPR.
  • Distributed Responsibilities: Administrative responsibilities are distributed among team members according to their roles, promoting collaborative management.

Operational Responsibilities

  • Program Management: Coordinating and overseeing the execution of research projects and educational programs.
  • Resource Allocation: Ensuring optimal use of financial, human, and technological resources to support ITCPR's activities.
  • Organizational Communication: Facilitating effective internal and external communication channels.

Financial Administration

  • Budget Management: Developing and overseeing the annual budget to align with ITCPR's goals and priorities.
  • Financial Transparency: Maintaining clear financial records, ensuring accountability and transparency in financial matters.

Human Resources

  • Talent Acquisition and Development: Managing the recruitment process and fostering continuous professional development and training of the team.
  • Performance Evaluation: Implementing regular performance assessments to support team growth and alignment with institutional objectives.

Compliance and Legal Affairs

  • Legal and Regulatory Adherence: Ensuring compliance with relevant laws and regulations, especially pertaining to non-profit status, international collaborations, and academic standards.
  • Contract and Agreement Oversight: Managing legal contracts, partnerships, and agreements effectively.

Technology and Infrastructure

  • IT Infrastructure: Overseeing the development and maintenance of IT infrastructure and digital platforms for virtual operations.
  • Technological Advancement: Ensuring that technological resources are up-to-date and aligned with the needs of the institution.

Policy Development and Implementation

  • Policy Crafting: Developing policies and procedures in line with ITCPR's mission and evolving needs.
  • Policy Evaluation and Update: Regularly reviewing and updating policies to ensure they remain relevant and effective.


Article VI: Amendments

Purpose of Amendments

  • Adaptability: Amendments ensure the Charter remains dynamic, relevant, and effective, reflecting the evolving needs of ITCPR and its community.

Initiating Amendments

  • Proposal by Leadership: Amendments can be proposed by the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, or Trustees.
  • Member Proposals: Members may also propose amendments, subject to initial review by the leadership.
  • Formal Submission: A written proposal detailing the suggested changes and rationale must be submitted for consideration.

Review Process

  • Leadership Review: The Executive and Co-Executive Directors, along with Trustees, review the proposed amendments.
  • Stakeholder Consultation: Consultation with key stakeholders, including research leads, lab managers, and members, for their input on significant amendments.

Approval Process

  • Leadership Vote: Approval of amendments requires a majority vote among the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, and Trustees.
  • Member Involvement: For substantial amendments, a broader consultation or vote among all members may be conducted to seek their consensus.

Notification and Implementation

  • Communication of Amendments: Approved amendments will be communicated to all members and stakeholders promptly.
  • Effective Date: Amendments become effective from a specified date, which will be announced along with the amendment details.

Documentation and Record Keeping

  • Amendment Records: Comprehensive records of all amendments, including the decision-making process and outcomes, will be maintained.
  • Charter Accessibility: The updated Charter, incorporating the amendments, will be made accessible to all members.

Periodic Review

  • Scheduled Charter Reviews: Regular reviews of the Charter will be conducted to ensure it aligns with current practices, laws, and the mission of ITCPR.


Article VII: Dissolution

Conditions for Dissolution

  • Dissolution Circumstances: The dissolution of ITCPR may be considered under extraordinary circumstances such as insurmountable financial challenges, fundamental changes in the institution's mission, or inability to operate effectively.
  • Consideration of Alternatives: Prior to initiating the dissolution process, thorough evaluation of all possible alternatives and potential impacts will be undertaken.

Proposal for Dissolution

  • Proposal Initiation: A formal proposal for dissolution can be initiated by the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, or a majority of the Trustees.
  • Details in Proposal: The proposal must clearly outline the reasons for dissolution, the proposed method for conducting it, and plans for asset liquidation and distribution.

Approval Process

  • Comprehensive Consultation: A detailed consultation process involving all members and key stakeholders to discuss the implications and gather input on the dissolution proposal.
  • Decision by Voting: The final decision on dissolution requires a unanimous vote from the Executive Director, Co-Executive Director, and Trustees, as well as a majority vote from the general membership.

Disposition of Assets

  • Asset Liquidation: In the event of dissolution, assets will be liquidated to settle any outstanding debts or obligations.
  • Charitable Allocation: Any remaining assets will be allocated to organizations with similar goals and missions, in accordance with legal requirements and ITCPR's objectives.

Notification and Compliance

  • Legal Obligations: The dissolution process will adhere to all applicable legal and regulatory requirements, ensuring a transparent and orderly conclusion of operations.
  • Stakeholder Notification: All stakeholders, including members, partners, and regulatory bodies, will be duly informed about the dissolution process and its progress.

Final Reporting and Documentation

  • Dissolution Documentation: Detailed records and documentation of the dissolution process, including financial settlements and asset distributions, will be maintained.
  • Summary Report: A final report summarizing the dissolution, its reasons, and the outcomes will be prepared and made available for institutional records.

Continuation of Mission

  • Legacy Preservation: Efforts will be made to preserve the legacy and impact of ITCPR, including sharing educational resources and research findings with the broader community to ensure the continuity of its mission.
