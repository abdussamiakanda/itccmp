Institutional Charter of ITCPR


Preamble


This Charter establishes the Institution for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research, henceforth referred to as "The Institution," dedicated to advancing the field of physics through research, collaboration, and education. This document outlines the foundational principles, governance structure, and operational policies of The Institution.

Article I: Name and Purpose


Name

  • Official Name: The official name of this entity shall be "Institution for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research".
  • Abbreviation: The Institution may also be referred to as "ITCPR" in all official documents and communications.

Purpose

  • Primary Objective: The primary objective of ITCPR is to advance the field of theoretical and computational physics through innovative research and educational initiatives.
  • Research and Innovation: To conduct and promote research in theoretical and computational physics that contributes to the global body of scientific knowledge, pushing the boundaries of understanding in the field.
  • Education and Training: To provide quality education and training programs in theoretical and computational physics, especially aimed at students and researchers from regions with limited access to such opportunities.
  • Collaboration and Community Building: To foster a collaborative and supportive community of physicists, encouraging the exchange of ideas and resources among researchers from different geographical and academic backgrounds.
  • Global Participation: To actively engage in the global scientific dialogue, participating in and contributing to international conferences, seminars, and workshops.
  • Accessibility and Opportunity: To democratize access to advanced research in physics, ensuring that talented individuals from underrepresented or underserved regions have the opportunity to contribute to and benefit from the advancements in the field.

Values and Principles

  • Commitment to Excellence: Upholding the highest standards of research and education in all endeavors.
  • Inclusivity and Diversity: Embracing diversity in all forms and striving for inclusivity in the scientific community.
  • Integrity and Ethics: Adhering to the highest ethical standards in all research and academic activities.
  • Innovation and Creativity: Encouraging innovative thinking and creative approaches to problem-solving in physics research.

Long-term Vision

  • Global Impact: Aspiring to make a significant impact on the global scientific community and contribute to the advancement of knowledge and technology in physics.
  • Sustainable Growth: Aiming for sustainable growth and development of the institution, adapting to the evolving needs of the scientific community and the world.

Article II: Legal Structure


Type of Entity

  • Classification: ITCPR shall be established and operated as a non-profit, non-governmental organization, dedicated to the advancement of theoretical and computational physics research and education.
  • Compliance with Laws: The Institution shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations of the jurisdiction in which it is registered. This includes adherence to legal standards pertaining to non-profit organizations, international collaborations, intellectual property, and academic research.

Registration and Legal Status

  • Jurisdiction: ITCPR shall be legally registered in Bangladesh, where it will maintain its primary legal address.
  • Registration Details: The Institution shall be registered with the Registrar of Joint Stock Companies And Firms (RJSC) as required by law, ensuring its recognition as a legitimate non-governmental educational and research entity.

Location and Operation

  • Principal Office: The principal office for the management of ITCPR shall be located in Bangladesh.
  • Virtual Operation: Recognizing the global and inclusive nature of its mission, ITCPR will primarily operate through virtual platforms, allowing for international participation and collaboration without geographical constraints.

Governance Compliance

  • Board of Trustees: Governance and oversight of the Institution shall be the responsibility of a Board of Trustees, which shall ensure that ITCPR operates within its legal and ethical boundaries and adheres to its mission and objectives.
  • Legal and Ethical Standards: The Board shall establish and enforce policies and procedures that uphold the highest legal and ethical standards in all activities of the Institution.

Fiscal Responsibility

  • Financial Management: ITCPR shall maintain transparent and responsible management of its finances, adhering to accounting standards and financial reporting requirements as stipulated by law.
  • Non-profit Integrity: As a non-profit organization, any surplus funds shall be reinvested into the Institution for the furtherance of its objectives, rather than being distributed to members or officers.

Amendments to Legal Structure

  • Procedure for Amendments: Any changes to the legal structure or status of ITCPR must be proposed by the Board of Directors and approved by a majority vote, following the procedures outlined in Article VI of this Charter.
  • Compliance with Changes in Law: The Institution shall adapt its legal structure as necessary to remain compliant with any changes in the laws and regulations governing non-profit organizations and educational institutions.

Article III: Governance


Co-Directors and Leadership

  • Co-Directors: ITCPR is led by two Co-Directors, who are jointly responsible for setting the vision, strategy, and overall direction of the institution.
  • Dual Roles: Given the early stage of the institution, the Co-Directors will also undertake operational roles, managing day-to-day activities and making key decisions.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Strategic Planning: The Co-Directors are tasked with developing and implementing ITCPR's strategic plan, including setting goals, defining research areas, and establishing training programs.
  • Resource Management: Overseeing the allocation and effective use of resources, including finances, human resources, and technology.
  • Research and Education Oversight: Guiding the research direction and ensuring the quality of research and education provided under ITCPR.
  • Internship Program Management: Designing and managing the internship program, overseeing the training of interns, and integrating them into the institution's activities.

Meetings and Decision-Making

  • Regular Meetings: The Co-Directors will hold regular meetings to discuss progress, deliberate on decisions, and plan future initiatives.
  • Consensus-Based Decisions: Decisions will be made through consensus between the Co-Directors. Structured discussions will be used to resolve any differences.

Inclusion of Interns

  • Participation in Discussions: Interns are encouraged to participate in specific meetings and discussions, providing valuable input and gaining insights into the institution's operations.
  • Feedback and Engagement: Regular feedback sessions will be organized with interns to gather their perspectives and involve them in shaping ITCPR's culture and activities.

Growth and Expansion of the Team

  • Future Team Expansion: As ITCPR expands, the governance structure will evolve to include additional members and potentially form a formal Board or specialized committees.
  • Adaptability: The governance structure is designed to be flexible, accommodating changes and growth in the institution's operations and scale.

Transparency and Communication

  • Open Communication: Ensuring transparent communication within the team, keeping all members informed and aligned with the institution's objectives.
  • Record-Keeping: Maintaining detailed records of decisions and discussions for transparency and future reference as the institution grows.

Article IV: Membership


Eligibility for Membership

  • General Criteria: Membership in ITCPR is open to individuals with a background in theoretical and computational physics.
  • Special Consideration: Priority given to candidates from regions with limited research opportunities.

Categories of Membership

  • Interns: Individuals in training contributing to research projects.
  • Research Members: Active participants in research and publication.
  • Affiliate Members: Collaborators and partners contributing to ITCPR’s mission.

Admission Process

  • Application and Review: Formal application process with review by Co-Directors.
  • Acceptance: Decision based on application review and institutional needs.

Rights and Responsibilities of Members

  • Participation: Right to participate in ITCPR’s activities.
  • Contribution: Expected to contribute positively to research and learning environment.
  • Code of Conduct: Adherence to ethical standards and respectful collaboration.

Termination of Membership

  • Voluntary Resignation: Members may leave by notifying Co-Directors.
  • Involuntary Termination: Termination by Co-Directors for breach of conduct or non-participation.

Updating Membership Policies

  • Policy Revisions: Membership policies may be revised as needed.
  • Communication of Changes: Updated policies communicated to members.

Article V: Administration


Administrative Structure

  • Leadership: Day-to-day administration overseen by Co-Directors.
  • Small Team Management: Administrative responsibilities shared among team members.

Operational Responsibilities

  • Program Coordination: Managing training programs and research projects.
  • Resource Management: Overseeing use of financial and human resources.
  • Communication: Managing internal and external communications.

Financial Administration

  • Budgeting: Preparing and managing the annual budget.
  • Financial Records: Maintaining accurate financial records and transparency.

Human Resources

  • Recruitment and Training: Overseeing recruitment and training processes.
  • Performance Management: Implementing performance evaluation for team growth.

Compliance and Legal Affairs

  • Regulatory Compliance: Ensuring adherence to laws and ethical standards.
  • Contract Management: Handling contracts and agreements.

Technology and Infrastructure

  • Digital Infrastructure: Development and maintenance of digital platforms.
  • Technology Upkeep: Ensuring technology supports institutional activities.

Policy Development and Implementation

  • Policy Formulation: Developing operational policies in line with mission.
  • Review and Update: Regular updating of policies to reflect institutional needs.

Article VI: Amendments


Purpose of Amendments

  • Adaptability: Process for making amendments to stay relevant and effective.

Initiating Amendments

  • Proposal: Amendments can be proposed by Co-Directors or members.
  • Submission: Written proposal outlining changes and reasons.

Review Process

  • Consideration: Review of proposed changes by Co-Directors.
  • Consultation: Consultation with members and advisors if necessary.

Approval Process

  • Majority Vote: Approval by majority vote of Co-Directors.
  • Member Involvement: Consultation or vote among members for significant changes.

Notification and Implementation

  • Communication: Communicating approved amendments to all members.
  • Effective Date: Announcement of the date when amendments take effect.

Documentation and Record Keeping

  • Amendment Records: Maintaining records of all amendments.
  • Accessibility: Making updated Charter accessible to members.

Periodic Review

  • Scheduled Reviews: Regular review of the Charter for necessary amendments.

Article VII: Dissolution


Conditions for Dissolution

  • Circumstances: Considering dissolution under specific circumstances.
  • Initial Evaluation: Evaluating alternatives before proceeding with dissolution.

Proposal for Dissolution

  • Submission: Written proposal for dissolution by Co-Directors or members.
  • Details of Proposal: Outlining reasons and method for dissolution.

Approval Process

  • Member Consultation: Discussing proposal with all members.
  • Voting: Unanimous vote of Co-Directors and majority vote of members required.

Disposition of Assets

  • Liquidation and Distribution: Distribution of assets after settling debts.
  • Charitable Distribution: Preference for organizations with similar missions.

Notification and Compliance

  • Legal and Regulatory Compliance: Adhering to relevant legal requirements.
  • Notification: Informing relevant parties about the dissolution.

Final Reporting and Documentation

  • Records: Maintaining records of the dissolution process.
  • Final Report: Preparing a summary report of the dissolution.

Continuation of Mission

  • Legacy and Impact: Efforts to continue the impact of ITCPR post-dissolution.
